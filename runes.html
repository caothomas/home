Runes

Runes are the most important items in the game. Having rare and powerful monsters is one thing, but without good runes, even a natural 3 star monster with good runes and the right rune set can defeat a natural 5 star monster. Now many people might be wondering , how does runes work and how can I get better runes? Well of course your not gonna get better runes just because you read this website. I am just gonna tell you where can you find good runes and what can you do to make your runes better. First, what are runes? Runes are items that drop in almost anywhere in the game. You can get them by completing challenges, clearing the scenario, TOA(tower of ascension), world boss, and even more that I have yet to name. Every monster have 6 rune slots. You may place any type of rune you want in the slots, but the slots are numbered from 1 through 6. Once you get a rune there will be a number beside the type of rune and the rarity of the rune. The number the rune has can only go into that rune slot. The effects of a runes will only work once its has been placed on the monster, or in other works engraved on the monster. Think carefully about what runes you want to engrave on the monster because removing them comes with a price. The better the rune is, as in number of stars, enhancements and rarity of the rune, the more mana stones it will cost to have it removed. If you do not want the rune and want to just replace the rune then go ahead. The rune will be lost and unrecoverable. Paying the price and having the rune removed will allow you to keep the rune and you can place the rune on another monster that you want. Now about the quality of the rune. Stars, number of enhancements, and rarity determine the quality of the rune as mentioned before. There are 3 parts of a rune, the main effect, sub effect, and the set effect. The main effect is as the name suggests, the main effect/buff the runes will provide for the monster it is engraved on. The number of stars will determine how good the main effect is. For example, a 1 star rune might have a main effect of +3 attack, while a 3 star rune might have a main effect of +7 attack. The max number of stars a rune can have is 6. The sub effect of a rune is extra effects/buffs that the rune provides but is not as good as the effect/buff would be if it was a main effect. For example, a 3 star rune can have a main attack of +7 attack while a 3 star rune with a main effect of +7 defense and a sub effect of +3 attack. The rarity of the rune determines how many sub effect the rune has. Runes can come in all kinds of rarities. There are normal, magic, rare, hero, and legend, having normal being the worst and legend being the best. Enhancing a rune will increase its main effect as well as sub effects. For every 3 enhancements the rune will go up a grade. If the rune was a normal rune, the with 3 successful enhancements it will become a magic rune. For every 3 enhancements will also give the rune an extra sub effect. The total number of sub effects a rune can have is 4. To find runes the better runes will drop according to the level of the stage. In the scenario/map, putting the settings to hell will allow you to get better runes. In the cairos dungeon, the stronger the boss, the better the runes that it will drop. Another way to get runes is the world boss. He has a chance to drop runes and since he is unlocked when the player finishes the scenario and has at least 10 monsters level 30 or over, that means that he will only drop runes with decent number of stars. Down below is a chart on all the different types of runes that can be obtained throughout the game.

Crafting runes

In this game, there are some special runes that can only be obtained by crafting. There is a building that is unlocked at level 19 called crafting building. The crafting building is where you can craft other buildings, a special monster called the Homunculus, and various items using crafting material. In the crafting building you can also craft runes. There are currently a total of 5 possible runes to craft. The runes consist of fight runes, determination runes, enhance runes, accuracy runes, and tolerance runes. All of these runes is a 2 set piece and grants all allies buffs. Fight runes grants 8% attack to all allies, determination runes grants 8% defense to all allies, enhance runes grants 8% HP to all allies, accuracy runes grants all allies 10% accuracy, and the final rune, tolerance runes grants all allies 10% resistance.

Rune Type Set pieces set effect Where it drops Description
Energy 2 +15% HP Energy runes can be obtained in Garen Forest and Giant's keep The increase in the monster's HP depends on its base HP. Having 3 sets of energy runes gives 45% total HP increase
Fatal 4 +35% attack Fatal runes can be obtained in Mt.siz and Dragon's lair. The increase on the monster's attack depends on its base attack.
Despair 4 +25% stun rate Despair runes can be obtained in Giant's keep. Despair runes will add 25% stun rate on all skills that deals damage (when a bomb detonates it will not trigger the stun effect). The monster will not have to have a stun effect on the skill that is uses for the skill to stun. For skills that does stun, the set effect will simply add an additional 25% stun rate. When a monster activates a multi-hit skill, the effect will only apply to the first hit.
Revenge 2 +15% Counterattack Revenge runes can be obtained in Faimon Volcano and Dragon's lair. Revenge runes will add 15% counterattack for each set of 2(so the max is 45% counterattack). This effect will activate when the monster is attacked by another monster and the monster that is being attacked will have a chance to unleash a counterattack, but the monster is only able to use its first skill when using a counter attack. The monster does not need to have a passive that will counterattack when attacked, but if the monster does, then the set effect will add on to the current percentage chance that the monster will counterattack.
Swift 4 +25% SPD Swift runes can be obtained in Mt. White Ragon and Giant's keep. Since all monsters have a base SPD, the set effect will add 25% of the monster's current base SPD.
Focus 2 +20% Accuracy Focus runes can be obtained in Talein Forest and Dragon's lair. Focus runes will grant the monster 20% accuracy in addition to the chance percentage the skill already has. Sometimes skills have an debuff effect that will activate on the enemy monster that it attacks with a chance percentage and the set effect will increase that chance.
Shield 2 Grants a shield that is proportional to 15% of the monster's base HP Shield runes can be obtained in Vrofagus Ruins and Dragon's lair. The set effect will not stack with having more sets. The set effect will not only grant the monster a shield that is proportional to its base HP, but allied monsters will also gain the buff.
Endure 2 +20% resistance Endure runes can be obtained in Tamor desert and Dragon's lair Endure runes will grant the monster an additional 20% resistance to the 15% base resistance that all monsters have when summoned
Blade 2 +12% crit rate Blade runes can be obtained in Kabir Ruins and Giant's keep Blade runes will give the monster an additional 12% critical rate to the 15% base crit rate that all monsters have when summoned.
Guard 2 +15% defense Guard runes can be obtained in Hydeni Ruins and Dragon's lair Guard runes will give the monster more defense proportional to 15% of the monster's base defense.
Violent 4 +22% extra turn chance Violent runes can be obtained only in Dragon's lair Violent runes will give the monster 22% chance to gain an additional turn. This set effect will not stack with passive and skills.
Will 2 + immunity for 1 turn Will runes can be obtained in Aiden Forest and Necropolis Will runes will grant the monster one turn of immunity at the beginning of the battle. This set effect will stack with each set(with 3 sets the monster will have 3 turns of immunity at the beginning of the battle).
Destory 2 -4% of 30% of the damage dealt to the enemy's max HP Destory runes can be obtained in chiruka's remains and Necropolis Destory runes are hard to understand. The effect is that everytime the monster attacks another monster and deals damage, the effect will take 4% of the 30% of the damage dealt and subtract that from the enemy's max HP. When it says subtract from the enemy's max HP means that when the enemy is revived or healed, they will not recover the HP that they lost from the set effect. This effect will only be able to take away a total of 60% of the monster's max HP, meaning that the monster will not be able to lose more than 40% of its max HP.
Rage 4 +40% crit damage Rage runes can be obtained in only Necropolis Rage runes will increase the monster's crit damage by 40%. Every monster has a has a base crit rate of 15%.
Nemesis 2 +4% attack power for every 7% of HP that the monster has lost. Nemesis runes can be obtained in Ferun Castle and Necropolis Nemesis runes will grant the monster 4% increase in attack power for every skill for every 7% of its HP that it has lost during the battle
Vampire 4 +35% life drain(basically life steal) Vampire runes can be obtained in Mt.Runar and Necropolis Vampire runes will give the monster 35% life steal. It will stack with skills and passives and it does not require the skill to have a life steal effect to use this set effect.
