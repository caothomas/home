Vegetation!

The vegetation in the Pacific Maritime ecozone is very extensive thanks to the 200-260 days of the growing season. There are 4 main forests in the Pacific Maritime ecozone. It consists of the Western Hemlock Forests, Mountain Hemlock Forests, Douglas Fir Forests, and Garry Oak Woodland Forests. Thanks to the heavy rainfall that the Pacific Maritime ecozone receives, it supports all the forests and in the forests include some of the worlds biggest, oldest, and tallest trees! For example, there is a Sitka spruce tree that measures 95 meters tall, and there are also some Red Ceder trees that are 2,000 years old or older. The western forest also has one of the worlds largest biomass. Here are some species of trees and plants that you can find in the Pacific Maritime ecozone:
